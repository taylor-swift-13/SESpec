LOOP INVARIANT (`Goal Invariant` failed):
  Frama-C reports two sub-classes; identify which one(s) appear in the errors above and apply the matching fix.

  (1) `Goal Establishment of Invariant ...`
      The invariant does not hold on entry to the loop (before iteration 1).
      Likely causes and fixes:
        - The clause is too strong for the entry state — weaken it to a form that the pre-loop initialization (and the function `requires`) actually establishes.
        - The required pre-state is not pinned down — add or tighten TARGET's `requires` so the entry state satisfies the clause (only if the body is reachable; do not introduce false preconditions).
        - The clause depends on values that the body has not yet computed at entry — restate it as an implication guarded by the loop's progress (e.g. `i > 0 ==> P(...)`) or split it into a base-case clause + an inductive clause.
        - Companion clauses are missing — add the auxiliary invariants whose conjunction is establishable, then write the goal clause as a consequence.

  (2) `Goal Preservation of Invariant ...`
      The invariant holds on entry but is not maintained after one iteration of the loop body.
      Likely causes and fixes:
        - A relation between loop-modified variables is incomplete — add the missing inductive equality / bound that the body actually maintains.
        - The clause is true under a stricter context the body does not preserve — weaken it, or add a stronger companion invariant that the body does preserve and from which the original follows.
        - Off-by-one on a counter / array index — adjust the bound (often `<` ↔ `<=`, or `var ≤ upper` ↔ `var ≤ upper + 1`) so the post-iteration state still satisfies it.
        - Frame variables are not pinned — add `var == \at(var, Pre)` invariants for variables the body provably does not modify, so the prover can reuse them.

  General rules (apply to both sub-classes):
    - Do not remove existing invariants; only add new ones or tighten failing ones.
    - Any new clause that references a logic function or predicate must have its definition in scope (see the predicate / logic-function rule).
    - Keep clauses simple and prefer the conjunction of small lemma-like invariants over a single large formula.
